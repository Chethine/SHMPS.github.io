Features

Diabetes & Heart Disease Prediction

We provide a detailed report of your overall health status, including personalized suggestions for lifestyle modifications that could potentially reduce your risk.

Harness the power of artificial intelligence and machine learning to predict your risk of diabetes and heart disease. Our algorithms analyze a variety of health indicators to provide comprehensive risk assessments.

Chronic diseases are consistently ranked among the leading causes of death on a worldwide scale. According to the World Health Organization, 71% of all fatalities globally are caused by chronic diseases such as diabetes, cancer, and cardiovascular disease (WHO). In Sri Lanka also most of people die due to this chronic diseases according to statistics .With the current economic situation of patients have to expend lot of money even identify the diseases such as doctor channeling, tests etc. As a solution they can use our system to predict their diabetes and heart diseases and condition of their health without showing test results to a doctor .So that expenses can be saved due to our system.

Health Management and Prediction System, abbreviated as HMPS. This system serves four important functions. They are able to identify and make predictions regarding chronic diseases when medical data or health reports are presented in the form of photos. Determine what the patient's most significant problem is. If the answer is yes, show some of the best doctors in your region, In the event that the patient's condition is considered to be life-threatening, hospitals and pharmacies should be provided. Compute the individual medical expenses that will be invoiced to the user's health insurance based on their day-to-day activities.

The system gives users the ability to input medical reports in the form of images. Then system gathers the relevant and important data in order to determine the current health status of the patient using parameters such as based on the liquor consumption, physical activities, smoking, and inheriting a predisposition to the disease. This is accomplished through a machine learning model that uses a linear regression model. Image processing will be used to extract the data from the medical reports, RP team will get the data in order to analyze them for illness diagnosis. When putting the model into action, you will be responsible for data collection, training of the model, and assessment of the model.

A component of image processing collected data on the prediction of diabetes. Next job is to find the most effective algorithm for predicting diabetes and heart diseases by making use of those data and achieving a high rate of accuracy. RP team will make use of the Support vector machine for predict diabetes and logistic regression model to predict heart diseases in order to obtain results that are more accurate. Patients are categorized within the system according to their state, and the system also decides whether or not the patient's condition is life-threatening. After that, the system does an analysis on the data that was entered and provides a percentage of healthy levels along with recommendations and shows doctors, medical centers, pharmacies and hospitals according to the location of the user.

Rp team will choose right machine learning model that, based on the datasets, can provide the most accurate prediction of the total cost of medical care. Using linear regression model for logistic regression, train the model by utilizing those data sets, and then validate the model to ensure that it is accurate and estimate individual health insurance-billed medical expenditures

The research team has identified a significant research gap in the area of study. Major risk factors for diabetes and heart disease include heavy alcohol and tobacco consumption, lack of physical activity, and the existence of the disease in one's parents. Nevertheless, the problem with existing studies and systems is that they do not take these aspects into account when predicting disease. In addition, the research team has determined that existing systems do not assess the criticality of the disease. These systems solely predict diseases and do not provide patients with any sort of recommendation. Current systems do not provide adequate privacy and confidentiality for medical records. People are increasingly concerned about the privacy of personal data, and there are rules such as HIPPA in place to protect the confidentiality of health records. The system's lack of privacy and confidentiality may discourage people from utilizing it.

Even though there have been several attempts to make secure health management and prediction systems for chronic diseases, there is still a big research gap in this area. One of the biggest problems is that these systems have not been tested and used in real life. Most of the research that has already been done in this area has focused on making and testing machine learning algorithms that can predict how a disease will progress. But these algorithms need to be tested in real-world settings and their ability to improve patient outcomes needs to be evaluated. Lack of focus on patient engagement and user experience is another research hole in this area. It is important to create machine learning algorithms, but it is just as important to make sure that patients are willing and able to use the system. The system's usability, user-friendliness, and ability to be accessed by patients from different backgrounds and demographics are key to its adoption and success. So, there needs to be user testing and an assessment of how well the system works for patients

Data interoperability and standardization are also problems that need to be solved. Chronic diseases require the collection and analysis of different types of health data, like vital signs, medication adherence, lifestyle factors, LIPID profiles, Full blood count reports and medical history. But these data are often stored in different formats and systems, which makes it hard to combine them onto a single platform. So, standards and protocols for data interoperability need to be made so that the system can effectively collect, analyze, and present patient data. Lastly, there's the problem of keeping patient data private and safe. Existing research in this area has emphasized the need for secure data storage and access control mechanisms, but new solutions are needed to reduce the risk of data breaches and unauthorized access. For example, the system could use blockchain technology to make sure that patient data is secure and can't be changed.

In conclusion, there is a big research gap when it comes to making and using secure systems for health management and disease prediction for chronic diseases. To fill these gaps, we need to do real-world implementation and evaluation, put patient engagement and user experience first, create data interoperability standards, and look for new ways to protect privacy and security.
